---
title: "Auto Labeling"
description: "Neatmail automatically categorizes incoming emails and applies labels directly in Gmail or Outlook in real time."
---

Auto labeling watches your inbox continuously and classifies each incoming email into a category. Labels are applied directly in Gmail or Outlook — no separate app or tab required. Classification runs in real time with 95%+ confidence, so your inbox stays organized the moment mail arrives.

## Built-in categories

Neatmail includes 9 built-in categories. Each email is matched to the most appropriate one based on its content and intent.

| Category | Color | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Action Needed | Red | Direct request to complete a task, approve, sign, submit, or decide. |
| Pending Response | Orange | Sender expects your reply (answer, clarification, confirmation), but no separate task execution. |
| Automated Alerts | Purple | System-generated notifications from tools/services (build, incident, status, reminder), not human conversation. |
| Finance | Blue | Money-related communication: invoices, receipts, billing, payments, expenses, payroll, taxes, statements. |
| Event Update | Dark blue | Calendar and meeting lifecycle updates: invite, reschedule, cancellation, RSVP, join details. |
| Discussion | Green | Human collaboration thread for context-sharing or brainstorming without a clear owner action. |
| Read Only | Gray | FYI or announcement content to read for awareness only; no reply or action expected. |
| Resolved | Dark green | Thread is closed: issue completed, question answered, or final confirmation already provided. |
| Marketing | Mauve | Promotional or sales outreach: newsletters, campaigns, offers, product updates, cold pitches. |

## Enabling and disabling labeling

Auto labeling is controlled by the watch toggle in your settings. When the watch is active, Neatmail processes incoming emails and applies labels. Turning it off pauses labeling without removing your label configuration.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Labels settings">
    Navigate to **Settings > Labels** in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Toggle the watch">
    Enable or disable the **Monitor Inbox** toggle to start or pause auto labeling.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Custom labels

Beyond the 9 built-in categories, you can create your own labels with a custom name, color, and description. Custom labels let you extend the system to match your specific workflow — for example, a label for a particular client, project, or team.

<Note>
  Custom labels require an active Pro subscription.
</Note>

To manage your labels, go to **Settings > Labels**.

## Where labels appear

Labels are applied directly in Gmail or Outlook as native labels or categories. You do not need to keep Neatmail open in a browser tab — the labels will be visible wherever you read your email.
