---
title: Draft Preferences
description: Customize how Neatmail generates AI reply drafts, including tone, signature, and when drafts are created.
---

Configure how Neatmail generates reply drafts at **Settings > Draft Preference**. These settings control when drafts are created, what instructions the AI follows, and how the draft text is formatted.

## Fields

### Enable Drafts

A toggle that activates or deactivates draft generation entirely. When active, Neatmail automatically watches incoming emails and drafts suitable responses based on your preferences.

<Warning>
  Draft generation requires an active subscription. The toggle is disabled if you do not have one.
</Warning>

### Draft Prompt

A free-text field (up to 1000 characters) where you provide personalized instructions for the AI. Use this to describe your communication style, goals, decision-making approach, or important details about your business or role.

<Tip>
  A detailed draft prompt leads to significantly better drafts. Include your name, role, preferred tone, and common response patterns you rely on.
</Tip>

### Email Signature

Paste your email signature here so it is automatically appended to generated drafts.

<Note>
  Copy your signature from Gmail settings rather than from a previously sent email. Copying from a sent email may include extra formatting that does not render correctly in drafts.
</Note>

### Draft Sensitivity

Controls when Neatmail generates a draft for an incoming email. Select one of the following options:

| Option | Behavior |
|---|---|
| `always draft` | Generate a draft for every incoming email. |
| `if known sender AND directly addressed` | Only draft when the sender is in your contacts and the email is addressed directly to you. |
| `if actionable` | Only draft emails that require a response or action. |
| `if actionable AND critical` | Only draft emails that are both actionable and marked as critical. |

### Font Size

Numeric field for the font size used in generated drafts. Accepts values between **8** and **72**.

### Font Color

Hex color picker for the text color used in generated drafts. Enter a hex value directly (e.g., `#000000`) or use the color picker to select a color visually.

## Saving changes

Click **Update preferences** to apply your changes. All fields are saved together.
