title: Labels

The Labels settings page is at **Settings > Labels**. From here you control how Neatmail categorizes your incoming emails, which built-in categories are active, and any custom labels you create.

## Inbox monitoring

The **Monitor Inbox** toggle enables or disables real-time email processing. When active, new emails are labeled as they arrive based on your selected category preferences.

<Warning>
  Inbox monitoring requires an active subscription. The toggle is disabled if you do not have a subscription.
</Warning>

## Category preferences

Choose which of the 9 built-in categories Neatmail should use to organize your inbox. At least 1 category must be selected to save preferences. Neatmail recommends enabling 5–6 categories that match your workflow.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Action Needed">
    Direct request to complete a task, approve, sign, submit, or decide.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Pending Response">
    Sender expects your reply (answer, clarification, confirmation), but no separate task execution.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Automated alerts">
    System-generated notifications from tools and services (build, incident, status, reminder), not human conversation.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Finance">
    Money-related communication: invoices, receipts, billing, payments, expenses, payroll, taxes, statements.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Event update">
    Calendar and meeting lifecycle updates: invite, reschedule, cancellation, RSVP, join details.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Discussion">
    Human collaboration thread for context-sharing or brainstorming without a clear owner action.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Read only">
    FYI or announcement content to read for awareness only; no reply or action expected.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Resolved">
    Thread is closed: issue completed, question answered, or final confirmation already provided.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Marketing">
    Promotional or sales outreach: newsletters, campaigns, offers, product updates, cold pitches.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Custom labels

Create labels beyond the built-in categories to fit your personal workflow.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the custom labels section">
    Scroll to the **Custom Labels** section on the Labels settings page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a label">
    Click **Create Label** and provide a name, color, and description for your new label.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable or disable the label">
    Use the checkbox next to each custom label to enable or disable it, just like built-in categories.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Delete a label">
    Open the actions menu next to a label and select **Delete**. A confirmation dialog will appear before the label is permanently removed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Gmail users can also import existing Gmail labels. Click the **Labels not in Gmail** button to browse and add labels that already exist in your Gmail account.
</Note>

## Folder preference

Choose whether matched emails are moved to a folder or label in your connected email provider (Gmail or Outlook). Configure this in the **Folder Preference** section on the Labels settings page.

## Saving changes

<Warning>
  Changes to label settings only take effect after clicking **Save Preferences**. Label settings require an active subscription to modify.
</Warning>
