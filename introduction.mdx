Neatmail watches your inbox in real time and organizes it for you. Incoming emails are classified and labeled directly in Gmail or Outlook the moment they arrive — no manual sorting, no batch jobs, no rules to maintain. When an email needs a reply, Neatmail generates a draft in your tone using GPT-4 Mini, ready for you to review and send.

Whether your inbox receives dozens or hundreds of messages a day, Neatmail keeps it structured so you can focus on the messages that actually need your attention.

## How it works

- **Connect your inbox.** Link Gmail or Outlook once via OAuth. Neatmail requests only the permissions it needs — no extra access.
- **Choose your categories.** Select from 9 built-in categories or create custom labels to match your workflow. Neatmail uses your selection to fine-tune classification.
- **Emails are labeled as they arrive.** When a new email hits your inbox, Neatmail classifies it in real time and applies the matching label directly in Gmail or Outlook.
- **Drafts appear automatically.** For emails that need a reply, Neatmail creates a draft in your inbox — ready to review, edit, and send.

## Built-in categories

Neatmail ships with 9 categories you can enable during onboarding or adjust later in **Settings > Labels**:

| Category | What it covers |
|---|---|
| Action Needed | Direct requests to complete a task, approve, sign, or decide |
| Pending Response | Emails where the sender expects your reply |
| Automated Alerts | System-generated notifications from tools and services |
| Finance | Invoices, receipts, billing, payments, and statements |
| Event Update | Calendar invites, reschedules, cancellations, and RSVPs |
| Discussion | Collaboration threads for context-sharing or brainstorming |
| Read Only | FYI announcements with no action or reply expected |
| Resolved | Closed threads where the issue is complete or answered |
| Marketing | Newsletters, campaigns, offers, and promotional outreach |

You can also create custom labels for categories specific to your workflow.

<Note>
  Neatmail requests only the minimum permissions needed to read email metadata, apply labels, and create drafts. No raw email content is stored on Neatmail servers.
</Note>
